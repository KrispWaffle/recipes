Sushi Recipe

Description

Sushi is a traditional Japanese dish that consists of vinegared rice combined with various ingredients such as seafood, vegetables, and sometimes tropical fruits. It is often served with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups sushi rice
  • 2 1/2 cups water
  • 1/3 cup rice vinegar
  • 3 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Nori (seaweed sheets)
  • Sushi-grade fish (e.g., tuna, salmon, yellowtail)
  • Cucumber, avocado, and/or other vegetables
  • Soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger (for serving)

Instructions

  1. Rinse the sushi rice in a fine-mesh strainer under cold water until the water runs clear.
  2. Combine the rice and water in a rice cooker and cook according to the manufacturer's instructions.
  3. In a small saucepan, combine the rice vinegar, sugar, and salt. Heat over low heat until the sugar and salt are dissolved.
  4. Transfer the cooked rice to a large bowl and gently fold in the vinegar mixture. Let the rice cool to room temperature.
  5. Place a sheet of nori on a bamboo sushi mat. Wet your hands and spread a thin layer of rice over the nori, leaving a 1-inch border at the top.
  6. Arrange your desired fillings (fish, vegetables, etc.) in a line across the rice.
  7. Roll the sushi tightly using the bamboo mat, starting from the bottom edge and rolling away from you.
  8. Use a sharp knife to slice the sushi roll into bite-sized pieces.
  9. Serve the sushi with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger.
